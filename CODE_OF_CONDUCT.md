# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We — maintainers and contributors of **transact-qda** — pledge to make
participation in this project a welcoming experience for everyone,
regardless of background, identity, level of experience, or academic
discipline. We want the project to be a space where methodological
disagreement and technical critique are possible without personal
attacks.

## Our standards

We commit to:

- Treating every participant with respect and patience.
- Welcoming newcomers and people at different levels of expertise.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback, including on
  methodology, ontology and design choices.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the research community
  it serves.
- Assuming good faith, and resolving disagreements through dialogue
  rather than escalation.

Behavior that is incompatible with participation in this project
includes any form of personal attack, harassment, or discriminatory
conduct, as well as the publication of others' private information
without consent.

This project adopts the norms articulated in the
[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).
Where this document is silent, that document governs.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — the GitHub
repository (issues, pull requests, discussions), any mailing lists or
chat channels associated with the project, and public events where an
individual is representing the project or its community.

## Reporting and enforcement

Concerns about conduct in project spaces can be reported privately to:

**<benjamin.joerissen@fau.de>**

Reports will be handled confidentially. Project maintainers are
responsible for clarifying the standards described here and will take
appropriate and proportionate corrective action in response to any
conduct they consider inappropriate. This may include a private
warning, a request to edit or remove content, a temporary restriction
from project spaces, or, in serious or repeated cases, a permanent
ban from the project.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good
faith may face consequences determined by other members of the
project's leadership.
